The Master Antioxidant: Why Glutathione Matters

Written by Dr. Sarah Hughes | March 22, 2026

Glutathione is often referred to as the "master antioxidant" because it is the only antioxidant found inside every single cell of your body. It is a simple molecule composed of three amino acids—cysteine, glycine, and glutamine—but its role in maintaining cellular health and systemic detoxification is unparalleled.

Detoxification and Resilience

The primary job of Glutathione is to neutralize free radicals and reactive oxygen species that cause cellular damage. It also plays a key role in the liver's detoxification pathways, binding to toxins like heavy metals and pollutants to make them water-soluble for elimination from the body.

Skin Brightening and Vitality

One of the most popular side effects of systematic Glutathione therapy is a brighter, more even skin tone. By inhibiting melanin production and reducing oxidative stress, Glutathione helps achieve a natural "glow" that topical creams simply cannot match.

"Without enough Glutathione, your cells oxidize much more quickly. It is essentially the 'rust protection' for your body."

Oral supplements of Glutathione are notoriously difficult for the body to absorb, as they are often broken down in the stomach before they can reach the bloodstream.
