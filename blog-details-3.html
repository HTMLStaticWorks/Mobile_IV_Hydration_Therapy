Marathon Prep: Optimal NAD+ Loading Protocols

Written by Dr. Sarah Hughes | March 20, 2026

Nicotinamide Adenine Dinucleotide (NAD+) is an essential coenzyme found in all living cells. It is fundamental to biological processes like metabolism and DNA repair. For elite athletes and endurance runners, NAD+ loading has become the gold standard for performance preparation and cellular age reversal.

Mitochondrial Fuel

NAD+ is critical for Mitochondrial health—the "powerhouses" of your cells. During intense training, mitochondrial efficiency can decline. Infusing NAD+ directly helps restore the cellular energy production cycles (the Krebs cycle), allowing for improved endurance, faster recovery times, and reduced muscle fatigue.

Sirtuins and Repair

NAD+ activates a group of proteins called sirtuins, which are the body's primary protectors against cellular aging and stressors. This triggers DNA repair mechanisms that are otherwise overwhelmed during peak athletic training cycles.

"What looks like a performance enhancer is actually a cellular restabilizer. We aren't just boosting; we are rebuilding."

Athletes typically undergo a 3–5 day loading protocol before major events to maximize cellular saturation. This results in sharper mental focus, sustained physical output, and a significant reduction in the typical inflammatory response following extreme exertion.
